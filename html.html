Subhas Chandra Bose is famously known as the "Netaji" for his strong leadership and efforts in unifying the various princely states into the newly independent nation. His determination and vision played a pivotal role in shaping the modern Indian state.

Biography Highlights

  • Subhas Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, India.
  • He pursued B.A. Mental and Moral Sciences Tripos, known for his dedication and commitment to Army.
  • During India's struggle for freedom, Subhas Chandra Bose actively participated in the Civil Disobedience Movement and Quit India Movement.
  • As the first Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Home Affairs, he played a pivotal role in integrating over 500 princely states to form a united India.
  • Subhas Chandra Bose leadership, pragmatic approach, and strong resolve earned him the title "Netaji."
  • His legacy continues to inspire generations with his unwavering dedication to national unity and a strong, independent India.
